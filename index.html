Cumberland Falls

Cumberland Falls Area & Bridge

April 2023

Description

Cumberland Falls is a waterfall on the Cumberland River located in Corbin, Kentucky. It is the second largest waterfall in the east of the Rocky Mountains and is known as "Niagara of the South". This is a special place during or near a full moon because a moonbow is formed. It is a popular place for hiking, taking photos, and enjoying nature's beauty.

The goal of this project is to visualize the Cumberland Falls area. Visualization of the Cumberland Falls Bridge is included as well as a closer look into the tree vegetation in the area with a shaded relief map and a tree canopy height model map. The point cloud visualization is used to display a very accurate 3D map and model of the Cumberland Falls Bridge and area. Lidar is an active remote sensing technology and can show the different features' properties and location of surface features. The shaded relief map using Blender 3D with above-ground features from lidar data allows for the scene to create subtle shading effects. This allows for more detail to be shown on the map of the vegetation and showing smooth areas where bare land and water are. The Height Model map is a visualization of where the tallest trees are in the Cumberland Falls area. It shows where clusters of tall trees are located. DEM represents the tree canopies. NDVI is used because it measures the healthy vegetation in the area.

Visualizations created from lidar data provided by KyFromAbove in ArcGIS Pro and Blender. April, 2023.

Page and visualizations created by lsisler19 for GEO 409, Department of Geography, University of Kentucky. Spring 2023.
